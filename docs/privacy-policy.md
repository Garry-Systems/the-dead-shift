# Privacy Policy — The Dead Shift

**Last updated:** 2026-07-05

The Dead Shift ("the game", "we", "us") is a single-player, offline mobile game. This
policy explains what data the game collects. Short version: **none.**

## Data collection

The Dead Shift does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data, device
identifiers, usage analytics, advertising identifiers, or any other information about
you or your device. The game does not require an internet connection to play and does
not make network requests during gameplay.

## Data storage

All game progress (save data, unlocked items, settings) is stored **locally on your
device only**, in the app's private storage area. This data:
- never leaves your device,
- is not accessible to us or to any third party,
- is deleted automatically if you uninstall the app,
- is not backed up to any server we control (any cloud backup of local save data is
  handled entirely by your device's own OS-level backup feature, e.g. Android's
  Auto Backup, which is outside our control and covered by that platform's own policy).

## Third-party services

The Dead Shift does not integrate any third-party SDKs — no analytics, no advertising
networks, no crash reporting services, no social login, no in-app purchase processors.
Nothing in the app communicates with any server.

## Children's privacy

Because the game collects no data of any kind from any user, this policy applies equally
regardless of the player's age. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone,
including children.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example, if a future update adds an online feature),
we will update this document and the "Last updated" date above before that update ships,
and describe the change plainly.
